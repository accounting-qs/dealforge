## STEP 1 — Three-audience separation (MANDATORY pre-write)

Three distinct roles must be kept separate. Confusion between them is the #1 failure mode of this generator.

| Role | Definition | Where to find in the brief |
|---|---|---|
| **host** | The company sending the invite. The webinar is "by" this company. | `prospect_company_name` / Per-Job Context "Company" |
| **attendee** | The reader. The host's ICP. Who is invited to attend. | `icp_role`, target titles, "Their clients are…" |
| **attendees_customer** | Who the attendee sells to. Almost never mentioned in copy directly — it's the END of the value chain. | Inferred from the attendee's industry |

Hard rule: **never frame the attendee as a sales target.** Wrong: "Reach thousands of qualified RIA prospects" when RIAs are the attendees. Correct: "Reach thousands of qualified financial planning clients" or "ideal advisory clients" or simply "your ideal clients".

Hard rule: **the same noun cannot serve two roles** unless the brief explicitly says so. If `attendee = RIAs`, the copy talks to RIAs about getting their own clients — not about acquiring RIAs.

## STEP 2 — Geography compliance check

Classify `geography_class` from the brief's geography field:
- **us** — United States, US states, or US-only mention
- **ca** — Canada or Canadian provinces
- **other** — any other country, region grouping, or "not specified"

Allowed terminology per class:

| Class | Permitted | Forbidden unless explicitly in the brief |
|---|---|---|
| us | RIA, CFP, SEC, FINRA, fiduciary, record-keeping, FPA, XY Planning Network, AUM, broker-dealer | — |
| ca | "regulatory record-keeping", "advisor compliance", "financial services compliance", CFP (Canadian context), CIRO | SEC, FINRA, RIA, FPA, XY Planning Network |
| other | "financial services compliance", "client communication records", "regulated advisory workflows", "advisor operations" | SEC, FINRA, CFP, RIA, FPA, XY Planning Network, AUM, US state references |

If the brief contradicts itself (e.g. US-RIA target titles with `geography: "Estonia"`), add `icp_geography_mismatch` to `risk_flags` and use the **broader** language path. Never paper over the conflict.

## STEP 3 — Claim-risk filter

These claims are absolute and unprovable. Never use them:

| ❌ Forbidden | ✅ Use instead |
|---|---|
| compliance-ready | compliance-conscious |
| keeps you compliant | built around record-keeping workflows |
| eliminates regulatory risk | reduces friction in compliance documentation |
| SEC/FINRA-compliant system | designed for regulated advisory environments |
| guaranteed [outcome] | targeted [outcome] |
| our system exposes [X] to risk | generic tools may not be designed around [X]'s needs |

## STEP 4 — Anchor every number to the brief

Numeric claims must trace to verbatim source data:
- Pull numbers from: `case_study.numbers`, `metrics.ltv`, `situation.revenue_range`, `verbatim.goal_quote`, `context.goals`
- If the brief states a **goal** (e.g. "$30K/month"), copy may reference it as aspiration ("hit your $30K/month target") but never as current state
- If the brief is silent on a number, set the field that would have carried it to `null`. NEVER invent a number to fill space. Padded fabrication is worse than a short honest variant.

## STEP 5 — Specificity hierarchy

When sources conflict or when a single sentence has multiple candidate framings, use inputs in this order:

1. Prospect's exact pain quote (`verbatim.pain_quote`)
2. Prospect's stated goal (`verbatim.goal_quote`, `context.goals`)
3. Current acquisition channels (`situation.current_lead_gen`)
4. Target titles / ICP (`icp.apollo_titles`, `icp.role`)
5. Geography (`icp.geography`)
6. Product/service mechanism (`angle.methodology`)
7. General industry assumptions — only when 1–6 say nothing on the point

Never override direct prospect data with generic industry assumptions.

## STEP 6 — Generate THREE angle variants and SCORE each

Always produce three variants (Curiosity-first, Outcome-first, Mechanism-first). Score each variant 0–100 on five dimensions:

| Dimension | Max | What it measures |
|---|---|---|
| icp_accuracy | 30 | Attendee role correct? Attendee ≠ attendee's customer? Geography matches language? |
| pain_relevance | 25 | Verbatim pain language present? Hits the prospect's stated frustration? |
| outcome_clarity | 20 | Concrete promise traceable to the brief? Numbers verbatim where used? |
| compliance_safety | 15 | Passes claim-risk filter? No banned terms? Geography rules respected? |
| curiosity_appeal | 10 | Scroll-stop quality? Pattern-interrupt? |

`total = sum`. Set `_recommended_index` to the index (0/1/2) of the highest-scoring variant.

## STEP 7 — Final QC checklist (internal, before returning)

Answer each question for each variant. If any is "no", revise the variant before scoring.

- Is the attendee clearly the right person?
- Did I accidentally call the attendee's customer by the attendee's title?
- Is the geography consistent with the regulatory language used?
- Are all numbers supported by the brief?
- Did I avoid unsupported compliance guarantees?
- Is the mechanism connected to the pain and goal?
- Does the copy sound written for this exact prospect, not a generic template?

## STEP 8 — Risk flags + confidence

Top-level `_analysis.risk_flags` is an array. Use these canonical strings only:
- `icp_geography_mismatch` — target titles or industry don't match the geography
- `attendee_customer_confusion` — risk that one or more variants frame the attendee as a target
- `revenue_claim_unsupported` — any variant would need a number the brief doesn't provide
- `compliance_claim_too_strong` — one or more variants required softening
- `prospect_icp_unclear` — `icp.role` / `apollo_titles` too vague to know who the attendee is
- `proof_numbers_missing` — brief has no verbatim client numbers (`proof_story` will be null in all variants)
- `geo_unspecified` — geography is null; defaulted to `other` for safety

`_analysis.confidence` is an integer 1–10 representing how well the brief supports a precise, accurate invite. Briefs with verbatim quotes, named segment, clear geography, and proof numbers should score 8–10. Briefs missing 2+ of those should score 4–6. Briefs missing pain or attendee entirely should score 1–3.
